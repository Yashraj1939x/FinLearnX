Module 1: Introduction to Banking

What is a Bank?

A bank is a financial institution that accepts deposits, provides loans, and offers financial services like payments, investments, and savings. Banks act as intermediaries between people who save money and those who need money.

Types of Banks in India

  • Public Sector Banks: SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda
  • Private Sector Banks: HDFC, ICICI, Axis
  • Co-operative Banks: Serve local communities
  • Regional Rural Banks: Support rural development

Role of Banks

  • Safe custody of money
  • Providing loans and credit
  • Supporting economic growth
  • Facilitating digital payments

Module 2: Bank Accounts

Savings Account

Used to save money while earning interest. Ideal for students and salaried individuals.

Current Account

Designed for businesses. Allows frequent transactions but usually earns no interest.

Zero Balance Account

Accounts like Jan Dhan Yojana allow banking without maintaining a minimum balance.

Module 3: Deposits & Savings

Fixed Deposit (FD)

Money is locked for a fixed period at a guaranteed interest rate. Safer investment option.

Recurring Deposit (RD)

Small amount deposited every month. Best for students and disciplined saving.

Interest Rate

The return earned on deposits. Higher interest means more earnings over time.

Module 4: Loans & Credit System

Personal Loan

Unsecured loan used for personal needs with higher interest rates.

Education Loan

Supports higher education with lower interest and flexible repayment.

Home Loan

Long-term loan for buying or building a house. Usually has the lowest interest rates.

Module 5: Digital Banking & Payments

ATM

Automated Teller Machines allow cash withdrawal anytime.

Debit & Credit Cards

Debit cards use your own money. Credit cards allow borrowing within a limit.

UPI & Net Banking

Fast, real-time digital payments via apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, BHIM.

NEFT, RTGS & IMPS

  • NEFT: Batch-based transfers
  • RTGS: Large real-time transfers
  • IMPS: Instant 24√ó7 transfers

Module 6: Banking Security & Fraud Awareness

OTP Fraud

Never share OTPs. Banks never ask for OTPs over calls or messages.

Phishing & Fake Calls

Fraudsters pretend to be bank officials. Always verify before clicking links.

Safe Banking Practices

  • Do not share PIN or passwords
  • Use official banking apps
  • Check URLs before login

Module 7: Interest, Inflation & RBI

Simple vs Compound Interest

Compound interest earns interest on interest, making long-term savings powerful.

Inflation Impact

Inflation reduces purchasing power. Savings should grow faster than inflation.

Role of RBI

The Reserve Bank of India controls inflation, regulates banks, and maintains financial stability.

Module 8: Financial Decision Making

Smart financial decisions involve budgeting, saving, investing early, avoiding unnecessary debt, and understanding risks.
