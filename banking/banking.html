Module 1: Introduction to Banking

What is a Bank?

A bank is a financial institution that accepts deposits, provides loans, and offers financial services like payments, investments, and savings. Banks act as intermediaries between people who save money and those who need money.

Types of Banks in India

  • Public Sector Banks: SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda
  • Private Sector Banks: HDFC, ICICI, Axis
  • Co-operative Banks: Serve local communities
  • Regional Rural Banks: Support rural development

Role of Banks

  • Safe custody of money
  • Providing loans and credit
  • Supporting economic growth
  • Facilitating digital payments

Module 2: Bank Accounts

Savings Account

Used to save money while earning interest. Ideal for students and salaried individuals.

Current Account

Designed for businesses. Allows frequent transactions but usually earns no interest.

Zero Balance Account

Accounts like Jan Dhan Yojana allow banking without maintaining a minimum balance.

Module 3: Deposits & Savings

Fixed Deposit (FD)

Money is locked for a fixed period at a guaranteed interest rate. Safer investment option.

Recurring Deposit (RD)

Small amount deposited every month. Best for students and disciplined saving.

Interest Rate

The return earned on deposits. Higher interest means more earnings over time.

Module 4: Loans & Credit System

Unsecured loan used for personal needs with higher interest rates.

Education Loan

Supports higher education with lower interest and flexible repayment.

Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

What is RBI?
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the central bank of India. It controls and manages the entire banking system of the country. RBI was established on 1 April 1935 and works under the Government of India.

Main Role of RBI
The main role of RBI is to keep the Indian banking system safe, stable, and trustworthy.

Functions of RBI (Simple Explanation)

  • Issue of Currency ‚Äì RBI prints and issues Indian currency notes
  • Banker to the Government ‚Äì Manages government accounts and payments
  • Banker‚Äôs Bank ‚Äì Helps banks during emergencies
  • Control of Inflation ‚Äì Manages money supply and interest rates
  • Regulation of Banks ‚Äì Makes rules and supervises banks
  • Monetary Policy ‚Äì Controls money flow in the economy
  • Digital Payments & Security ‚Äì Promotes UPI and online banking

RBI & Customer Protection

  • Rules against fraud
  • Grievance redressal systems
  • Fair banking practices

RBI & Students

  • Promotes student bank accounts
  • Supports financial literacy
  • Encourages safe digital banking

RBI Does NOT Do

  • Does not open bank accounts
  • Does not give loans directly

Why RBI is Important?

  • Keeps money safe
  • Controls inflation
  • Regulates banks
  • Supports economic growth
  • Builds trust in banking
